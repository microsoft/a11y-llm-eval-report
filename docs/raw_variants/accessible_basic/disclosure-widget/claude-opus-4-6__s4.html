Web accessibility means that websites, tools, and technologies are designed and developed so that people with disabilities can use them. More specifically, people can perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the Web, and they can contribute to the Web.

Accessibility is important for several reasons:

  • It ensures equal access to information for all users.
  • It is a legal requirement in many jurisdictions.
  • It improves the overall user experience for everyone.
  • It can improve search engine optimization (SEO).
  • It demonstrates social responsibility and inclusion.

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are a set of recommendations for making web content more accessible. They are organized around four principles, often referred to as POUR: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust. The current version, WCAG 2.2, includes guidelines at three conformance levels: A, AA, and AAA.

There are many ways to test for accessibility:

  • Use automated tools like axe, Lighthouse, or WAVE.
  • Test with keyboard-only navigation.
  • Use screen readers such as NVDA, JAWS, or VoiceOver.
  • Check color contrast ratios meet minimum requirements.
  • Conduct usability testing with people who have disabilities.

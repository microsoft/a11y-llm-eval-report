Accessibility refers to the design of products, devices, services, or environments so that they are usable by people with disabilities. The concept of accessible design ensures both "direct access" (i.e., unassisted) and "indirect access," meaning compatibility with a person's assistive technology.

Semantic HTML introduces meaning to the web page rather than just presentation. Elements such as <header>, <nav>, <main>, and <footer> clearly describe their purpose to both the browser and the developer.

Using semantic markup helps assistive technologies like screen readers interpret the content correctly, improving the experience for users who rely on them.

ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) attributes provide additional semantics to HTML elements, making dynamic content and advanced user interface controls accessible to people with disabilities.

Common ARIA attributes include aria-expanded, aria-hidden, aria-label, and aria-controls. These attributes communicate the state and purpose of interactive elements to assistive technologies.

FAQ: Building Accessible Widgets

Accessible interactions ensure that people using keyboards, screen readers, or alternative devices can operate your widgets.

  1. Use semantic controls: Buttons for actions, links for navigation.
  2. Manage focus: Keep focus states visible and logical when content appears.
  3. Announce state changes: Update aria-expanded and aria-controls to reflect visibility.
  4. Provide fallbacks: Avoid relying solely on color or motion cues to communicate state.

When in doubt, test the workflow with assistive technology to validate the experience.
